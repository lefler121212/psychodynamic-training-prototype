Session 01

Setting the Frame & the Therapeutic Relationship

A 34-year-old engineer in his second session of therapy arrives five minutes late and almost immediately asks whether you have children and how long you have been practising. Your task is to hold the frame while remaining curious and warm.

Clinical Vignette

Marcus, 34, an engineer, arrives five minutes late to his second session. He drops his bag, scans the room quickly, and within minutes says: "Do you have kids? And how long have you been doing this?" He makes the questions sound casual, almost offhand — but his eyes watch you carefully for your reaction.

Question 1 of 3
1 Marcus asks whether you have children. Which response best holds the frame while remaining warm?
Question 2 of 3
2 You gently decline to answer and Marcus becomes briefly irritated: "It's just a question — I'm not asking anything weird." What is the most therapeutically useful response?
Question 3 of 3
3 Marcus settles slightly and says: "I guess I like to know who I'm dealing with." Which response best uses this opening?

Session 02

Free Association & Listening

A 28-year-old graduate student, articulate but self-editing, is invited to free-associate in her fourth session. Your task is to follow the drift of her associations without summarising or redirecting — and to tolerate silence.

Clinical Vignette

Priya, 28, a graduate student, settles into her chair. You invite her to say what comes to mind without editing herself. She begins: "A novel I'm reading — it's about a woman who disappears — my mother used to disappear when she was angry, not literally, she'd just go somewhere else — actually this room is colder than last week, isn't it—" She stops abruptly and looks at you. "Sorry, this is probably all over the place."

Question 1 of 3
4 Priya has just offered a chain of associations — novel, disappearing woman, her mother, the room's temperature — and then apologised. What is the best response?
Question 2 of 3
5 Priya continues associating, then hits a sudden silence and says: "Actually — I think I wanted to talk about my dissertation instead." How do you respond?
Question 3 of 3
6 Priya sits quietly for almost a full minute and then says: "I keep thinking about whether my mother knows I'm in therapy." What do you do?
